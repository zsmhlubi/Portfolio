Projects

Student Survey and Support App

The application served as a survey and support tool designed to facilitate the administration of school-relevant surveys and enhance the lecturer's ability to assess students' understanding of course content. Two teams collaborated on the development of this application, with my team specifically assigned to the creation of the checklist functionality.

Within our scope of work, we focused on establishing the checklist creation process. This involved ensuring that administrators and lecturers, excluding students, could generate checklists and access analytical insights derived from checklist outcomes. Conversely, students were provided with the capability to respond to the checklists and offer comments on the thematic or content aspects. These comments were intended for use by the administrative team in order to improve the overall effectiveness of the checklists.

GitHub link : Student Survey and Support App

Wits Academy

This initiative served as a means of establishing an online educational platform, facilitating the acquisition of diverse courses by students and providing individuals with the capability to share the instructional content they wish to disseminate.

Our team was assigned the task of developing both a mobile and web application for this purpose. The primary objective was to enable instructors to upload course materials, while concurrently allowing students to access and study these resources. Additional functionalities were incorporated into the application to enable features such as announcements, user profiles, and more.

Throughout the course of this project, we employed black box testing techniques to enhance our comprehension of software development. This strategic testing approach proved instrumental in refining our understanding of the intricacies involved in the development process.

GitHub link : Wits Academy

Parallel Programming

I was tasked with developing a program to implement the parallel scan algorithm and a Parallel Graph Algorithm, specifically Dijkstra’s Single Source Shortest Path (SSSP) Algorithm. For both algorithms, I was required to implement both a serial processing algorithm and a parallel processing algorithm. In the case of parallel processing, I employed OpenMP. Additionally, I created another parallel algorithm using MPI to optimize the runtime of the algorithms that were initially executed in series.

The shell is designed to function in the following fundamental manner: upon entering a command in response to its prompt, the shell initiates the creation of a child process responsible for executing the entered command.
